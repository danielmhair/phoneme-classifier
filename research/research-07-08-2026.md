## How my recommendation maps onto what you have

Epic 1 + Epic 2 *is* the model half of the architecture: Wav2Vec2/WavLM feature extraction → CTC head → ONNX export, plus your temporal brain (smoothing/hysteresis/confidence gating) which is essentially a hand-rolled streaming stabilization layer — that's the part most people never build and it pairs naturally with CTC's frame-level emissions. So the suggestion isn't "start this recipe," it's three deltas to what exists:

1. **The data delta.** Your 87.00% / 85.35% numbers are on your own recordings — presumably not a broad sample of 4–8-year-olds. Everything in the child-ASR literature says rankings and absolute accuracy shift on real child speech (note that Wav2Vec2 beat WavLM on your data, while Block Medin et al. found the opposite on 5–8-year-old readers — dataset-dependent, and exactly what your Epic 4 bake-off harness should re-litigate on child data before you lock a backbone).
2. **The scoring delta.** Your CTC head emits phoneme sequences; the game knows the target word. Adding a CTC-GOP/forced-alignment scoring layer on top (the logit-based GOP variants from Interspeech 2025 are nearly drop-in for any CTC model) converts "what did they say" into "was /b/ in *bat* acceptable" — that's the product feature.
3. **The runtime delta.** Yes: sherpa-onnx C API or direct ONNX Runtime in UE5, not NNE. Your temporal brain logic is portable C++ math; sherpa-onnx gives you the audio plumbing, VAD, and endpointing around it.

One thing to verify before UE5 integration, because it's the biggest potential gotcha I see in the docs: **your CTC ONNX models are listed at ~5MB, but a Wav2Vec2/WavLM base backbone is ~95MB even INT8-quantized.** That strongly suggests the exported ONNX is the LSTM+CTC head only, with the 768-dim features computed by the HuggingFace backbone in Python upstream. ## Allosaurus: honest verdict

Allosaurus is the 2020-era universal phone recognizer — a pretrained model covering 2000+ languages based on the ICASSP 2020 "Universal Phone Recognition with a Multilingual Allophone System" work, with a simple CLI (`python -m allosaurus.run -i file.wav`), language-inventory restriction via `--lang`, approximate per-phone timestamps, CPU inference, and a built-in fine-tuning workflow that shows improvements with as few as 10 training instances. It's tiny (11M parameters) and trivially real-time.

But the 2025 ZIPA paper benchmarked it head-to-head, and the numbers settle your question: on English phone recognition, Allosaurus (11M params) scores 4.18/6.21 PER on the two English eval sets, while wav2vec2-lv-60-espeak gets 4.09/4.26 (at 300M), and ZIPA-T-small — at 65M params — gets 1.17/2.14. So even on clean *adult* English, Allosaurus is ~3x worse than a model in its own size class; on child speech (and especially on Sky/Cassie-style speech), expect the gap to widen, because SSL-era representations are exactly what buys robustness to child acoustics, and Allosaurus predates them. ZIPA trained Zipformer-based CTC and transducer variants on IPAPack++, 17,132 hours of normalized phone transcriptions, though its own error analysis admits persistent limitations on sociophonetic diversity — i.e., it's also adult-trained and would also need child fine-tuning.

So: **use Allosaurus as a one-afternoon zero-shot baseline column in your Epic 4 bake-off** — `pip install allosaurus`, loop it over your folders, map its IPA output to your 37 classes, and you have an independent reference point plus a cheap "second opinion" labeler (its timestamps and verbatim phone output are handy for flagging clips where it disagrees with your CTC, which is a decent uncertainty signal for the SSD re-annotation work above). **Don't make it a production candidate.**

The more interesting move: if you want a phone-recognition *checkpoint to fine-tune* rather than building CTC heads on WavLM/Wav2Vec2 features, ZIPA-CR-small (64M, CTC) is the modern version of what Allosaurus was — and it's a Zipformer, the same architecture family sherpa-onnx exports and streams natively. That gives you a third contender for the bake-off with a built-in runtime story: your current W2V2-CTC vs. wav2vec2-espeak fine-tune vs. ZIPA-CR fine-tune, all judged on hold-out-Chloe.
