❯ Can you explain to a 12 year old what LOSO is?                                
● Quick detour to answer that, then I'll get back to the augmentation work.

  LOSO = "Leave-One-Speaker-Out." Here's the kid version:

  Imagine you're teaching a robot to recognize sounds kids make, like "b" or
  "s." You have recordings from 5 different kids. If you teach the robot using
  all 5 kids' voices, and then test it using recordings from those same 5 kids,
  of course it does great — it already heard their voices!

  That's like a teacher giving you the exact same questions on the practice test
  AND the real test. You'd ace it, but it wouldn't prove you actually learned
  anything — you might've just memorized the answers.

  So instead, LOSO does this: pick one kid — say, Chloe — and hide all of her 
  recordings. Teach the robot using only the other 4 kids' voices. Then test it
  on Chloe, a voice it has never heard before. That's a real test of whether the
  robot actually learned the sounds, or just memorized specific voices.

  Then you repeat that for every kid — hide Callie and test on her, hide Dan and
  test on him, and so on — so every kid gets a turn being "the surprise voice."
  That's the "leave one out, rotate through everyone" part.

  We cared about this especially for Chloe because she's the only kid in the
  group who's the actual age of the kids this app is really for — so her score
  is the best clue for "will this actually work for a real kid using the game?"
