  Q2: How should the CTC "decoding" fix actually work вҖ” greedy or beam search?

  Quick plain-language version of what this means: a CTC model doesn't output
  "the answer" directly вҖ” it outputs a probability guess for every tiny 
  time-slice of the audio (e.g. 50 slices for a half-second clip), including a
  special "blank" (silence/nothing) symbol. The current broken code averages
  those 50 guesses together, which is like blurring 50 photos into one and
  asking what it shows. The fix is to instead read the slices in order, collapse
  repeated identical guesses into one, and drop the blanks вҖ” that's "greedy
  decoding." Beam search is a fancier version that keeps several competing
  guesses alive at once and picks the best overall sequence, rather than
  committing greedily slice-by-slice вҖ” it mainly pays off for longer, ambiguous
  sequences (like whole sentences).

  My recommendation: greedy decoding for this pass. Your phonemes are short,
  isolated single sounds (not sentences), so there's minimal ambiguity for beam
  search to resolve, and greedy is much simpler to implement, debug, and
  explain. Beam search becomes worth revisiting later if/when you evaluate CVC
  sequences (see next question) and see it actually collapsing distinct phonemes
  incorrectly.

  Does greedy-only sound right for now, or do you want beam search in scope from
  the start (e.g. because you already suspect greedy will blur similar-sounding
  phonemes)?
