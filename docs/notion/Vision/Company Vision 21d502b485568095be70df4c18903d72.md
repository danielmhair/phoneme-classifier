## 🛤 **Overall Vision**

- Children explore a magical open world (like *Fluffy Branch Haven*) guided by a magical orb.
- The **map expands dynamically** based on the child's **literacy growth** — not time or XP.
- Literacy follows **Science of Reading** stages:
    
    → **Phonemic Awareness** → **Decoding/Phonics** → **Fluency** → **Comprehension**.
    
- Even earlier zones **evolve** ("backfill") with new content as the child’s skills improve.
- Reading feels like part of the adventure — not separate lessons.

---

## 🎮 **Micro-Activities by Literacy Stage**

### 1. **Phonemic Awareness (no letters yet)**

- **Sound Hunt**: Find objects matching a sound (e.g., /m/ → mushroom).
- **Rhyming River**: Find two objects that rhyme.
- **Syllable Drumming**: Beat a drum for each syllable heard.
- **Magic Echo Cave**: Oral phoneme substitution games (e.g., change "sat" to "mat").

### 2. **Decoding & Phonics (letters + simple words)**

- **Alphabet Road Signs**: Recognize letter sounds on signposts.
- **Word Building Bridge**: Assemble letters to form a word to rebuild a bridge.
- **Treasure Chest Locks**: Match sounds/words to open chests.
- **Environmental Reading**: Signs and labels on world objects ("pig" pen, etc.).
- **NPC Dialogue Sound-Out**: NPCs gradually use decodable written speech.

### 3. **Fluency (short sentences)**

- **Signpost Sentences**: Read short instructions on signs to perform actions.
- **Timed Delivery Quests**: Read notes to deliver packages correctly under gentle time pressure.
- **Interactive Story Dialogue**: NPCs engage in short, story-driven reading conversations.
- **Word Wizard Duel**: Read sentences aloud quickly to "deflect" magic spells.

### 4. **Comprehension (paragraphs and beyond)**

- **Quest Journals & Letters**: Read story clues and act based on understanding.
- **Library Knowledge Quests**: Read short informational texts tied to mini-missions.
- **Puzzle Instructions and Riddles**: Solve riddles or follow multi-step written instructions.
- **Reflective Dialogue**: Choose responses showing understanding of stories or quests.

---

## 🗺 **Dynamic World Expansion and Backfill:**

- **Unlock new zones** only after mastering reading skills at each stage.
- **Backfill old zones** with richer content (e.g., new signs, quests, deeper story elements).
- Early areas like "Phoneme Fields" stay meaningful — evolving with the child's skills.
- **Procedural elements** (like hidden caves or special books) can generate automatically based on player needs.
- Always maintain **engagement**, **exploration**, and **wonder** through the literacy journey.

---

## ⚙️ **Core System Components You’ll Need:**

| Component | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Phoneme Classifier** | Listens to child’s speech, detects phonemes/words. |
| **Phoneme Parser** | Breaks words into phonemes, checks if the child knows them. |
| **Adaptive Manager** | Chooses right words/sentences based on skill level. |
| **Student Model** | Tracks skills learned, struggles, mastery levels. |
| **Procedural World Manager** | Unlocks new areas, backfills older ones dynamically. |
| **Feedback System** | Gives hints, encouragement, and corrections as needed during activities. |

---

## 📚 **Important Science of Reading Principles Built-In:**

- **Explicit skill building** (phonemic awareness first, then decoding, then sentences).
- **Systematic progression** (structured, not random difficulty jumps).
- **Lots of repetition and review** (especially phonics patterns).
- **Decodable text matched to child's skill** (no guessing strategies).
- **Fluency grows from connected text**, not isolated word drills.
- **Comprehension tied to action** (using reading to play and solve quests).

# Overall Focus for the Game

| Layer | What to Focus On | How to Think About It |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 🧠 **Core Engine** | Phoneme classification + learning profile | Reusable. The backbone of everything else. |
| 🎮 **Mini-Games** | One polished micro experience (e.g., guess the sound, speak the word, unlock a door) | Playable demo, emotional feedback, real use |
| 🌍 **World Layer (Optional)** | Simple hub with one place to go next | No need for full open world now — just the idea of "progressing" is enough |

🔑 **Treat each mini-game as a testable cell** of your bigger world. Each one should:

- Be replayable
- Build one clear skill (e.g. CVC words, blends, etc.)
- Update the learner's profile

## ⚒️ 3. Build Tools While You Build Content

To future-proof and reduce burnout:

| Tool | Why It Helps |
| --- | --- |
| ✅ **Game/Activity Template** | So every new game doesn’t start from scratch |
| ✅ **Learning Profile System** | Keeps track of what the learner knows (skills, phonemes, etc.) |
| ✅ **Content Generator** (even if it's just CSV for now) | Makes building new levels/words/sentences fast |

Even 1–2 hours invested into reusability pays off *big time* later.

| Engine Layer | Example |
| --- | --- |
| ✅ Phoneme classifier | Says child said "ʃ" — update profile for /ʃ/ |
| ✅ Mini-game logic | “This door needs a /th/ sound to open” → blocked until mastered |
| ✅ Content system | “Generate a sentence with words using only mastered phonemes” |
| ✅ XP system | Reward child for progress, not perfection |
| ✅ Pacing | Detect burnout/repetition, suggest a break or shift focus |

## 🎮 Mini-Game Generation as Part of World Generation

### When the world is generated:

- It uses the **child’s learning profile** to:
    - Select which phonemes, words, or skills are ready to be practiced
    - Choose from a pool of **15+ mini-game types**
    - Spawn those mini-games in context (e.g. “There’s a phoneme forest” → game for /ʃ/, /f/, /tʃ/)
    - Ensure **variety, challenge balance**, and **skill progression**
