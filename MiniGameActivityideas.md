# Mini-game Activity Ideas

---

## 0. Name places - take the name of the place and have them spell the name with sounds.

“Welcome to Fluffy Branch Haven! A place of safety and learning. Let's spell it! What are the sounds of fluffy?

### 1. **Glowing Orb – Speed Recall Trials**

🧠 **Purpose:** Build *automaticity* through fast-paced, visual-audio recall

🌍 **How it works:**

- A glowing orb in the environment hovers above an enchanted stone circle. It calls out a sound (e.g. “/g/!”).
- The player must find and tap the matching letter glowing among many floating letters.
- **The faster the child responds**, the more glowing particles or butterflies they earn.
- Optional: letter orbs “twinkle out” if ignored too long (adds urgency).
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Repeats letter-sound pairing dozens of times in just a few minutes
- Feedback: “That’s /g/ – just like in *goat*! Awesome speed!”
- Speed bar shows improvement across rounds
- Expandable: use 3–5 target letters for focused repetition before switching set

---

### 2. **Echo Pool – Multi-Sensory Recall Ritual**

🧠 **Purpose:** Combine visual imprinting, physical motion, and internal voice

🌍 **How it works:**

- Magical pool shows one letter at a time (e.g. “g”) shimmering on the water
- Player walks up and performs 3 actions in sequence (just like your chin method!):
    1. **Touch the letter** glowing on a stone (physical)
    2. **Touch their avatar’s chin** (you can animate this or trigger sound)
    3. **Say or select the sound** (or echo it back via game voice)
- Then the pool glows brighter each time they “lock it in”
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Option to “close eyes” (screen fades to black) and the game says “Can you still see the letter? What does it say?”
- Can later use those letters in stealth words ("What word hides *g*?")
- Optional repetition: player does this 3–5 times per session with one letter
- Visually stunning, calming, repeatable—*like a memory ritual*

---

### 3. **The Sound Garden – Plant It to Remember**

🧠 **Purpose:** Repetition through visual and emotional connection

🌍 **How it works:**

- Each letter has a **matching flower type**.
- To grow the flower, the player must do **a repetition cycle**:
    1. Tap the glowing seed marked “g”
    2. Hear the letter's sound
    3. “Chin tap” moment (simulated with animation or button)
    4. Then repeat the sound 3 times
- After 3 good cycles, a beautiful flower blooms
- Later, these flowers become collectible (each one associated with a sound)
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Repetition without boredom, because it’s connected to beauty and growth
- The player builds a visual **memory garden** of mastered sounds
- Optional "memory review walk" lets the player visit flowers and hear their sounds again

---

### 4. **The Memory Grove – Firefly Challenge**

🧠 **Purpose:** Use timing and attention to strengthen memory pathways

🌍 **How it works:**

- Fireflies are buzzing around. When one lands, it glows and *briefly* shows a letter.
- The player must **tap it and say the sound** quickly
- The faster and more correct the recall, the brighter the firefly glows before flying off
- Mistake? A soft wind blows the firefly away with a kind voice saying the correct sound
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Engages visual memory and timed response
- Adds pressure in a gentle way, increasing automaticity
- Short bursts = great for ADHD kids
- Could combine with mini-story: “These fireflies help store your memories! Help them glow brighter!”

---

### 5. **Letter Echo Drums – Rhythm Repetition**

🧠 **Purpose:** Use rhythm and repetition to lock in sound associations

🌍 **How it works:**

- Magical drums appear in a small clearing, each marked with a letter
- The game plays a rhythm: tap-tap-*pause-*tap, while saying “/g/… /g/… … /g/”
- The child matches the rhythm by tapping the correct drum
- Each correct match strengthens the beat and unlocks more instruments or colors
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Repetition through musical entrainment
- Multi-sensory + audio + motion = deeper encoding
- Fun and expandable for full-letter sound sets later
- “G is for Groove!” could be the tagline 😄

---

### 6. **Crystal Letters – Guided Visualization Quest**

🧠 **Purpose:** Reinforce visual memory + inner speech

🌍 **How it works:**

- Oriana gives your daughter a guided visualization challenge:

> “The crystal shows the letter. Can you close your eyes and still see it?”
> 
- The screen darkens, and the letter softly glows in her mind (visual prompt)
- Game prompts her to describe the shape or trace it from memory (in VR or PC)
- When she recalls it + says the sound, the crystal bursts into light
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Mirrors your “describe what the letter looks like” success exactly
- Builds mental image + inner verbal loop
- Encourages independent recall instead of just recognition
- Can become a series of “mental mastery” challenges for tough letters

---

### 7. **Flashlight Forest – Find and Say**

🧠 **Purpose:** Speed up visual identification + recall

🌍 **How it works:**

- Forest is dark. The player has a glowing lantern
- She hears a sound (“Find /d/”)
- Must quickly scan the area and point the light at the right letter glowing faintly in the woods
- Fast find = stronger light beam + coin or creature pops out
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Makes rapid sound-to-letter mapping feel like a treasure hunt
- Encourages focus, scanning, immediate connection
- Optional timer mode for challenge or story mode for ease

---

### 8. **Memory Match Paths – Spaced Recall Adventure**

🧠 **Purpose:** Encourage **spaced repetition** with subtle reintroduction

🌍 **How it works:**

- On the trail, the player finds letters she’s “already learned”
- Each is embedded in a different object (e.g., “g” etched in a rock, burned into wood)
- When she taps it, it says “Do you remember me?”
- She then must say the sound or click the correct one from 3 choices
🌀 **Retention Boosters:**
- Brings back old letters from earlier in the game, spaced apart
- Reminds her that *memory needs refreshing*, gently
- Reinforces through context and surprise (makes her feel clever for remembering!)
